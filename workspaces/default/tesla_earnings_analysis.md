# Tesla Financial Analysis (2021-2024) and Q3 2025 Forecast

## Overview
This report analyzes Tesla's (TSLA) Q3 financial performance over the last four years, focusing on revenue growth, net income, and vehicle delivery trends.

## Historical Q3 Performance
| Quarter | Revenue ($B) | Net Income ($B) | Deliveries |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Q3 2021 | 13.76 | 1.62 | 241,391 |
| Q3 2022 | 21.45 | 3.29 | 343,830 |
| Q3 2023 | 23.35 | 1.85 | 435,059 |
| Q3 2024 | 25.18 | 2.17 | 462,890 |

## Analysis Findings
- **Revenue Growth:** Tesla has shown consistent year-over-year revenue growth in Q3, though the explosive growth seen in 2022 has normalized to a more sustainable single-digit percentage trajectory as of 2023-2024.
- **Profitability:** Net income has fluctuated due to price adjustments, rising operating expenses (AI/FSD investments), and varying margins. Q3 2024 demonstrated resilience with $2.17B in net income, supported by regulatory credits.
- **Deliveries:** Delivery volumes remain the primary driver of top-line performance, with growth correlating closely with production capacity expansion.

## Q3 2025 Projection
Based on a historical moving average growth rate of approximately 24.19% (influenced heavily by the high growth in 2022), the projected revenue for Q3 2025 is estimated at **$31.27 Billion**. This projection assumes consistent growth in energy storage and steady vehicle delivery momentum. 

*Note: This is a mathematical projection based on historical trends and does not account for potential macroeconomic shifts, changes in interest rates, or unexpected supply chain disruptions.*
